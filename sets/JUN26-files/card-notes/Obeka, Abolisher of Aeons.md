???.?? One card, Obeka, Abolisher of Aeons, states that spells and abilities don’t resolve during a step. During that step, spells and abilities remain on the stack but the step continues as though the stack were empty. Those spells and abilities will resolve normally once that step is over.

Rulings:

Since the cleanup step can only occur during the ending phase, getting one or more additional cleanup steps outside of the ending phase actually means you get an additional ending phase for each of those cleanups. In those ending phases, the end step will be skipped.

Here’s what happens when you activate Obeka’s ability. First, the ability itself immedaitely bypasses any other abilities on the stack just like how a mana ability does; it can’t be responded to. Then, if the player whose turn it is has more cards in hand than their maximum hand size, they discard cards equal to the difference. Then, simultaneously, all damage marked on permanents is removed and all "this turn" and "until end of turn" effects end. Then, any state-based actions are performed and any triggered abilities that are waiting to be put on the stack — including "beginning of the next cleanup step" triggers are put on. Then, if either of those two things actually happened, players gain priority to cast spells.

Obeka’s ability essentially freezes both combat and the stack while the extra cleanup step is happening. Spells and abilities can still be added to the stack, and combat will pick up from where it left off after the cleanup step is taken.
